Land version vs cruise version

How this one is different

This is the alternative if the two of you would rather not sail. You lose the island-hopping (Ibiza, Tunis, Malta, Sicily) that only a ship reaches, and you take on more of your own logistics. You gain a lower cost, more days, and full flexibility.

What you give up

The ship's easy rhythm, one unpack for the week, and the ports you can only reach by sea. You now book hotels, trains, and transfers yourself, and you carry your bags between cities. Peak-August rooms on the Amalfi Coast and in the Cinque Terre run high.

What you gain

Your cost drops to about $5,100 from about $11,200, since there is no cruise fare. You get roughly two weeks instead of one on the water, more hiking (Path of the Gods plus the Cinque Terre), and you can change any day on the fly.

The cruise version

If you would rather have the ship, the cruise plan is the other page. It keeps the island ports and gives Mom a comfortable week with one unpack. Open the cruise version to compare side by side.

Your days together

Five days together on the Amalfi Coast

Mom flies in and out of Naples and stays in one hotel in Sorrento the whole time, so she never repacks. Sorrento is flat and walkable, and the scenic days come to her by boat or private car. Here is the plain version for the two of you.

One easy base

Sorrento sits on a cliff over the bay, with traffic-free lanes, plenty of restaurants, and an easy pace. She unpacks once and stays put for five nights. Naples airport is about 1 to 1.5 hours away by private car (about €90 to €120, door to door).

Days that take no effort

A private driver runs the Amalfi Coast day, a boat runs the Capri day, and a guide runs Pompeii. She sets the pace and never chases a bus. You attend Sunday Mass together in Sorrento on Aug 9 before she flies home.

Where it drifts

The big swing is the coast hotels in peak August. You cut the most by sharing the Sorrento room with Mom during her five days, and by booking the Cinque Terre rooms early.

Versus the cruise

The cruise version runs about $11,200 for your share, since a luxury cabin is the bulk of it. This land trip runs about $5,600 for double the land nights, but you handle all the logistics yourself.
